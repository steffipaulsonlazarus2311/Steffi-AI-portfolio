Common Process
01
Brief

Define the emotional and conceptual territory. Establish tone, intention, and the questions the film should pose.

02
Ideation & Script

Develop a sequence of ideas into a loose narrative logic. Write prompt-scripts rather than traditional screenplays.

03
Storyboarding

Sketch compositional rhythm, shot order, and emotional pacing before generation begins.

04
AI Generation

Iterative generation with evolving prompts. Curate rigorously — most frames are rejected in service of the few that are true.

05
Compilation

Sequence, grade, and score the selected frames into a cohesive film. Rhythm is established in the edit.

Deloitte

Deloitte – Data Trust & AI Foundations

A cinematic exploration of transitional moments — airports at 3am, empty corridors, half-lit rooms. Each frame was generated from precise emotional cues, assembling a dreamlike narrative of belonging and absence.

Visual Development

Scene 01 — Empty terminal, dawn light

Scene 02 — Corridor, soft flicker

Scene 03 — Waiting room, dusk

Scene 04 — Rain on glass

Scene 05 — Escalator, upward pan

Scene 06 — Fluorescent hum

Scene 07 — Exit sign, red bleed

Scene 08 — Still water, final frame

Prompt Evolution
Iteration 01
I

"Empty airport corridor at 3am, fluorescent light, photorealistic." — Too literal. The space read as surveillance footage, not cinema.

Iteration 02
II

"Deloitte airport, melancholic." — Better atmosphere, but colour felt arbitrary and unanchored.

Iteration 03
III

"Deserted terminal, amber underlight, drift of fog at floor level, Tarkovsky-esque, golden hour memory." — Emotion leads; description follows.

What I Learned

Studying the script deeply helped me understand the importance of data trust and governance in AI systems and how it impacts real-world decision-making.

Breaking the script into visuals and generating storyboards for each scene taught me structured planning and visual storytelling for technical content.

Working closely with the client highlighted the value of attention to detail, especially in maintaining consistent color themes and visual language across the project.

Experimenting with color choices, like proposing a golden theme for harmony instead of strict red/green coding, improved the visual coherence and gained client approval.

Iterating based on client feedback reinforced the importance of flexibility, communication, and collaboration in delivering polished, approved outputs.

Overall, I learned how to translate complex AI concepts into clear, engaging visuals while balancing creativity and technical accuracy.

AI generation rewards emotional framing over descriptive precision. Once I began prompting with feeling states rather than visual facts, the outputs shifted dramatically toward coherence. Deloitte – Data Trust & AI Foundations taught me to write for the model's intuition, not its vocabulary.

Chroma

Chroma Protocol

A brand film conceived as a living synesthetic system. Colours don't decorate — they narrate. Each hue carries a distinct behavioural signature, and transitions between them form the grammar of the story.

Visual Development

Scene 01 — Red pulse, zero point

Scene 02 — Indigo bloom, slow

Scene 03 — Gold thread emergence

Scene 04 — Collapse to white

Scene 05 — Frequency scatter

Scene 06 — Cyan interval

Scene 07 — Brand mark resolve

Scene 08 — Fade to black

Prompt Evolution
Iteration 01
I

"Abstract colour film, red and blue, commercial." — Completely generic. No signature, no tension. Results borrowed without contributing.

Iteration 02
II

"Chromatic abstraction, ink diffusion in liquid, slow motion, score-driven." — Closer, but still illustrative rather than conceptually driven.

Iteration 03
III

"Colour as a living syntax. Each hue a verb. Monochromatic bursts sequenced like a musical phrase. Clinical yet ecstatic." — The film found its language.

What I Learned

Treating colour as a narrative system rather than a stylistic choice was the conceptual breakthrough. The film became an argument about perception — that we can be taught to read chromatic sequences the way we read sentences. The hardest part was resisting the decorative impulse at every turn.

Atlas

Hollow Atlas

An invented cartography. Hollow Atlas maps territories that cannot exist — subterranean coastlines, cities built on the undersides of clouds. Every frame is a landscape from a world that dreams itself into coherence.

Visual Development

Scene 01 — Inverted mountain range

Scene 02 — Bioluminescent river

Scene 03 — Cloud-base city plan

Scene 04 — Aerial survey, drone

Scene 05 — Cave mouth at sea

Scene 06 — Latitude meridian

Scene 07 — Tidal compression

Scene 08 — Origin point, still

Prompt Evolution
Iteration 01
I

"Fantasy landscape, aerial, otherworldly." — Too familiar. Results borrowed from existing genre conventions without generating new geography.

Iteration 02
II

"Impossible terrain, photogrammetry style, scientific accuracy, unfamiliar biome." — More specific, but the scientific frame constrained the needed strangeness.

Iteration 03
III

"A geography that remembers being something else. Inverted logic. Terrain as memory. As if cartography were mourning." — The model responded to the poetic register.

What I Learned

Worldbuilding through AI requires building a conceptual grammar before generating a single image. The most distinctive landscapes emerged not from describing what they looked like, but from articulating what logic governed them. Internal coherence precedes visual specificity.

Signal

The Quiet Signal

Silence has a texture. This documentary film portraits the lived experience of quiet — not as absence, but as a presence with weight and dimension. Synthetic imagery was used precisely because no camera can see what silence looks like from the inside.

Visual Development

Scene 01 — Pre-dawn interior

Scene 02 — Dust in beam of light

Scene 03 — Breath condensation

Scene 04 — Hands at rest

Scene 05 — Window, no wind

Scene 06 — Clock without motion

Scene 07 — Long corridor, listening

Scene 08 — Signal end

Prompt Evolution
Iteration 01
I

"Quiet room, documentary style, minimal." — Produced stock photography. Nothing communicated what silence feels like as an interior state.

Iteration 02
II

"Interior silence, slow time, visible air, contemplative, soft." — Better register, but composition remained too resolved. Silence should feel unfinished.

Iteration 03
III

"The texture of silence before it breaks. Rooms holding their breath. Space at the edge of sound." — This version produced frames you could feel.

What I Learned

The Quiet Signal confirmed something suspected across all four projects: AI generation is fundamentally a translation medium. The quality of your filmmaking lives entirely in the quality of your thinking. The model is only as imaginative as the concepts you bring to it.
